Welcome!

Learn a little bit about the Frederick County Mathcounts Open!


Middle school students are preparing to solve math challenges during the countywide MATHCOUNTS Open competition on Saturday, January 4, with some moving on to the MATHCOUNTS Regional on Saturday, February 8. Both events will take place at Governor Thomas Johnson (TJ) Middle, with registration beginning at 8:30 a.m. and competition running from 9 a.m. until 12:30 p.m.

More than 100 middle school students are expected to compete in MATHCOUNTS this year. In regional competition, schools are limited to teams of four. Winning teams go on to the state tournament at Johns Hopkins University in March.

“These competitions are the culmination of before- and afterschool clubs that have met for months with the help of highly dedicated teachers who want to see our nation’s math achievement reach number one,” says TJ Middle MATHCOUNTS Coach Joe Daly.

In the event of inclement weather, the January 4 event will take place January 11, and the February 8 event will take place February 15.

For more information, visit the official MATHCOUNTS website.

Frequently Asked Questions

Get the answers to some frequently asked questions about the FCMO.


Sprint Round: focuses on speed and accuracy. Students have 40 minutes to complete 30 math problems without a calculator.

Target Round: focuses on problem-solving and mathematical reasoning. Students receive 4 pairs of problems and have 6 minutes to complete each pair, assuming the use of a calculator.

Team Round: focuses on problem-solving and collaboration. Students have 20 minutes to complete 10 math problems, assuming the use of a calculator. Only the 4 students on a school's team can take this round officially.

Countdown Round: focuses on speed and accuracy. Students have a maximum of 45 seconds per problem without a calculator. This round is optional at the school, chapter and state level.

Some light breakfast snacks, including some coffee, will be provided for the contestants, coaches, parents, and volunteers.

The results from the Frederick County MATHCOUNTS Open are not official. In other words, they will not determine the individuals who qualify for the state competition in March. Instead, the Frederick Count MATHCOUNTS Open serves as an opportunity for students and coaches to get the feel of the MATHCOUNTS competition and better determine students' strengths and weaknesses under a simulated environment.
